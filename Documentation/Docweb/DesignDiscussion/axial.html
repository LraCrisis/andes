Ontology for axial vectors

Mail from Joel Shapiro, 1/30/01 to Pitt Andes group


Workbench/Pedagogy Issues - Joel

We might bring these up for discussion with the usna folk.

Should we change the treatment of axial vectors, like angular velocity, angular momentum, torque, magnetic field?

In a two dimensional world these are really scalar (actually pseudoscalar) quantities, which we are representing by the z component of a vector. But describing such a quantity in a two dimensional world in term of a vector magnitude and a polar angle seems very artificial. I think the workbench would be clearer if it said counterclockwise/clockwise or out of/into the plane, rather than phi = 0 or 180. Also, it is very awkward to write out equations in terms of the magnitude and phi, as witness my complaints on the torque problems some time ago.


 Joel Shapiro, April 2, 2001
