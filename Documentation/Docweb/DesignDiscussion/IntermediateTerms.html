Intermediate terms during PSMs

(as of 3/21/01)

Kurt says:

When applying NSL, it is not necessary to draw a net force or define a net force variable. However, students sometimes do it.  Similarly, when applying conservation of energy, student sometimes define variables for total mechanical energy even though they could easily write the Law in terms of a sum of individual energies instead.  These are cases of students making intermediate terms explicit even though they don't have to.  Here's the pedagogical policies I think Andes should follow:
  • If students produce intermediate terms, then Andes should mark them correct.
  • Whats-next help should not suggest that the students draw or define intermediate terms.  For instance, we don't want Andes to suggest drawing a net force as part of NSL.  Such drawings are often confusing to students, because they haven't mastered the distinction between individual and net forces.
  • Suppose the student produces an incorrect version of an intermediate term (e.g., draws a zero net force when the net force is actually zero, as one student did.).  Andes could either recommend not bothering to produce the term, or it could remedy the error.  I think it should remedy the error, because it is more important to learn the properties of physical quantities than to learn the most efficient problem-solving procedure.
One approach to implementing these policies is to include choices in the PSM paths such that one choice defines a net force or other intermediate term, and the other choice does not.  The choice that does not define the intermediate term is first, so that the whats-next help system will prefer it whenever it has a choice.  There may be other techniques for achieving the same effect (e.g,. optional goals?) that need to be thought through.
