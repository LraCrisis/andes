Generic Sign Error

(as of 5/19/01)

If the student's entry is an equation

  • that contains a term ?term
  • and ?term is not inside a product (i.e., we want to transform T+m*g=0 to T-m*g and not to either T+(-m)*g=0 or T+m*(-g)=0.)
  • and replacing ?term by (- ?term) makes the equation correct
then the hint sequence is
  • (hint string (point) "Check your signs.")
  • (hint string (bottom-out) ...)
Clearly, this bug has to be tested after more specific sign errors have been tested.

M030072 Eq-USNA 12-50

In the problem with the jumping kangaroo, which seeks the initial velocity of the kangaroo given the height of the jump, after defining variables and drawing a FBD, the student entered d_y=2.5, a_y=-9.8, vfinal_y=0 and then the incorrect equation “vinit_y^2=2*a_y*d_y – vfinal^2”  The closest matching correct equation is a combination of  vfinal_y^2 = vinit_y^2 + 2*a_y*d_y and vfinal_y=vfinal, neither of which have been entered.  The student probably meant to enter -vinit_y^2 = 2*a_y*d_y-vfinal^2, but dropped the minus sign on the left side of the equation when she moved terms across the equal sign in order to isolate the sought variable.  Thus, the most likely diagnosis is that this is an algebraic manipulation error; its unlikely that she believes the kinematics equation is vfinal_y^2= -vinit_y^2 + 2*a_y*d_y.
  • · IH notices algebra manipulation error and through critique tries to get student to figure out error on their own.
  • · RO notices same problem (sign error) but tells student there is a sign error.
  • · Both IH and RO address v_f^2=v_o^2 + 2*a_y*d_y
  • · Andes suggests swapping variables instead of noticing the sign problem.
  • KVL: The student's intended entry is premature.  Students should write a compo version of kinematics before trying to substitute anything in.  Thus, Andes should say, "Although the equation you've written is incorrect, the closest matching correct equation would be premature.  It combines two or more primitive equations.  Try writing your equations in textbook form without doing any substitutions.  Then combine them.  Although this takes longer, you probably will not make the error that you've made here."  The bottom out hint would be: "Write vfinal_y^2 = vinit_y^2 + 2*a_y*d_y."
Since the only magnitude is vfinal and it is zero, the MissingNegSignOnVectorMag error handler will not yield a correct equation.  Thus, only this generic sign error handler will apply. It can put a negation on vinit_y^2 or on 2*a_y*d_y.  Also, because vfinal is zero, it could put a negative sign on the whole right side of the equation.  Thus, there are 3 possible applications of this error handler to the equation.  Which application we choose doesn't matter because they all end up with the "premature equation" tutorial turn.
