Using time points as durations

(as of 3/26/01)

If the student's entry is an equation

  • that has one or more time points in it (where a time pint is defined initially by Andes)
  • and substituting durations for the time points creates a correct equation
then the hint sequence is
  •  a training card hint (e.g,.  “Time points stand for specific clock times, such as January 1, 2000, 9:34 am.  To express the duration of a time interval, you should define a duration variable. Although you can also express a duration by subtracting two time points (e.g., T1-T0), this can lead to messier equations.”)
  • bottom-out hint

Shrike Eq-USNA 5-52

In the problem where a bullet is fired and hits a gong, after defining variables (no FBD), the student entered "2s=(vb*T1)+(vs*T2)" as the first equation.  The target equation has s1 instead of s (s is undefined), T1-T0 instead of T1 and T2-T1 instead of T2.
  • IH points out that student meant s1 instead of s.  Then IH asks student to express "t" (presumably, the length of time between the firing the bullet and hearing the sound) in terms of T0, T1 and T2.
  • RO says, "you don't know T1 and T2.  You only know the differences between time points, such as T2-T0=0.731."
  • Andes points out that there are no values for T1 and T2 and thus any equation with these variables will be marked as incorrect.
  • KVL: Since this is a speed-distance-duration problem and not kinematics, the student doesn't have to draw vectors and axes first.  Thus, Andes should just teach the use of time points.
Let's assume the student has corrected the undefined variable and asked what's wrong again.  Andes notes that the equation has time points in it, so it tries substituting durations for the time points until it finds that 2*s=(vb*t01)+(vs*t12) is a correct equation.  Indy analysis finds that it is a compound of s1=vb*t01 and s1=vs*t12.  SG analysis indicates that they are not premature.  Andes thus has to decide between teaching the target equations or a slip interpretation.  The use of time points instead of intervals is marked as a highly likely slip
