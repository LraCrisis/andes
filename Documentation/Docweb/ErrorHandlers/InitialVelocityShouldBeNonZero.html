Initial velocity should be non-zero

(as of 3/27/01)

Students are given so many problems where the initial velocity is non-zero that they sometimes assign zero-length initial velocities even on problem that state that the initial velocity is not zero.

If the student's entry is a velocity

  • that is zero length
  • and its time specifier is a point that is the initial time for a kinematics PSM
  • and changing it to non-zero lenth (plus adding a correct direction) makes it correct
then the hint sequence is
  • (hint string (point) "Is <body> at rest at <time>?")
  • (hint string (bottom-out)  "Read the problem statement again and draw a non-zero velocity in the correct direction.")

kcmae at 10-43

A soap box derby car starts with initial velocity of 1.00 m/s and accelerates to 15.0 m/s over 62.0 seconds.  What is its average acceleration?  The student draws a zero-length initial velocity, but it should be non-zero.
