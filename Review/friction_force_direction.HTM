Friction Force

When an object contacts another surface and either tries to move or actually moves across it, the surface exerts a friction force on the object.

This friction force is parallel to the surface and opposes the motion of the object relative to the surface.

When the object actually moves across the surface, as in this problem, the force is called kinetic friction force.

Friction is caused by the microscopic bonding and interlocking of the tiny bulges and indentations of one surface with another.

One way of visualizing this is shown in the animation below. In it, $BODY$ is represented by the purple rectangle. When $BODY$ moves in the $DIR$ direction across $FRICTION-AGENT$, $FRICTION-AGENT$'s tiny dents are bent towards the $DIR$ direction. In reaction, the bent dents push back on $BODY$'s dents in the $FRICTION-DIR$ direction. This is the kinetic friction force that $FRICTION-AGENT$ applies on $BODY$.
